# Sprint 3 — Inventory Decision Retrospective

## What worked well

- contracts and policy were frozen before UI implementation;
- strict product identity and units prevented silent cross-module joins;
- deterministic scenarios made every recommendation reproducible;
- the backend remained a validated read adapter rather than a second policy engine;
- expandable stage navigation preserved each module's mental model;
- a unified generator and gate protected the full product instead of isolated parts.

## Challenges

- the repository contains small synthetic evidence rather than governed retail data;
- historical stock observations cannot prove live inventory freshness;
- the in-app browser policy blocked real responsive captures;
- the default Vite process path can be restricted while pinned direct bundle
  compilation and the live HTTP smoke remain available.

## Improvements adopted

- explicit evidence states and limitations travel into the public resources;
- adversarial fixtures cover identity, units, staleness and malformed reports;
- the final demo binds official artifacts to hashes and exercises real HTTP;
- deployment readiness distinguishes a runnable local release from production approval;
- release documentation and traceability are checked as repository artifacts.

## Carry-forward

Any future product phase must begin from real-data governance and deployment
discovery. It must not silently convert observed averages into forecasts,
priority indices into probabilities, or review suggestions into autonomous
orders.
